By nature, human learning is a multi-modal process. We combine numerous streams of information through our senses, both to directly learn from a single given modality itself, and to compliment the information present within other another modality. Consider someone playing the drums. With only visual information present, you may learn that hitting a snare or a hi hat causes some event to take place, but you do not have the audio information to truly understand what that event is, only that it is happening. Similarly, with only audio information, you can learn that there are indeed two distinct sounds are taking place, but you would be unable to understand the phsyical attributes that are causing that sound to take place, only that they are different.

This work was presented at the International Computer Vision Summer School (ICVSS) 2022.
